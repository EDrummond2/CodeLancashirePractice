Life Episodes

Within a fortnight, I organised a 7000-letter mail-shot to the Human Resources contacts at large employers based in the UK, asking them to put forward prosepctive students for a prestigious course at Lancaster University Management School. Managing the project involved sourcing and managing additional staff, ordering stationary, and organising contact lists, print runs and setting up the task chain at each session.
Doing a research degree using a new programming language offered opportunities and motivation to hit the ground running. Once lockdowns meant also unexpectedly homeschooling children, I had the chance to show my skills in prioritisation and independent research. Continuing to work efficiently and successfully despite unsupervised for months at a time, during which I had no contact as supervisors were dealing with their own pandemic-related issues. I managed to not only continue to effectively solve problems, but also develop all the project's validation methods and data visualisation.
During my time working at the University of Cambridge, I acted as the bridge between the fly lab and the wet lab, leading to a role in the project's steering committee. Both groups had their own signficant expertise but needed to understand the work and limitations of the other team. I created booklets on PCR for the fly team so they could understand the processes in the wet lab, and helped negotiate timetabling issues to enable the teams' work run smoothly.
